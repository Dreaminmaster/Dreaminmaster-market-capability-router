# Findings

- Users often cannot search for a service because they do not know the service exists.
- Profession terms can recover useful providers that service-name searches miss.
- Marketplace prices frequently represent consultation, deposit, or placeholder amounts rather than total cost.
- Independent review is often a better first purchase than full execution.
- Marketplace language is time-sensitive and requires verification dates and confidence.
- Candidate quality cannot be inferred from star ratings alone; deliverable clarity and permission requirements are more important.
